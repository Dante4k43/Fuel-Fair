How the Fair Load Score Works

The Fair Load Score helps drivers quickly understand the profitability of a load.

What the Score Measures

The Fair Load Score evaluates a load using three main factors:

  • Net profit after estimated fuel costs
  • Net revenue per mile
  • Fuel efficiency of the truck

How Net Profit Is Calculated

Fuel Fair estimates fuel cost using:

Fuel Cost = (Miles ÷ MPG) × Fuel Price

Net profit is then calculated by subtracting fuel cost from the load rate.

Net Revenue Per Mile

After estimating fuel costs, Fuel Fair calculates how much money the load produces per mile of travel.

Net $/Mile = Net Profit ÷ Miles

Score Interpretation

  • 80-100 – Excellent load
  • 60-79 – Fair load
  • 40-59 – Low margin
  • Below 40 – Likely unprofitable

Driver Preferences

Drivers can set a target net $/mile in their account preferences. Fuel Fair will flag loads that fall below the driver's target.

Important Note

The Fair Load Score is designed to be a quick decision aid, not a replacement for a driver's own judgment or cost structure.
